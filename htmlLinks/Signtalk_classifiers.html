CEU'S: An intensive workshop on how to improve ASL skills with the use of classifiers. Participants will learn different kinds of classifiers that identifies elements, locative, descriptive, and limb. Activities will give participants opportunities to practice and enjoy the learning process. The goal is to become familiar and comfortable with classifiers!
This class will be conducted in ASL
EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES:
  • Rules of ASL Classifiers
  • Differentiating between types of classifiers such as limb, element, descriptive and locative
  • When and how to use Classifiers to share information about the surroundings; and/or as part of a story i.e., to set the scene or to share characteristics etc.

Miriam Morrow (CDI)has 18 years of experience teaching American Sign Language (ASL) to children, adults, college students, family members of deaf children, and professionals. She appears in several ASL educational CD-ROMS for children and adults. She is currently teaching ASL at New York University and is an ASL consultant nationwide where she provides professional development on a variety of topics.
