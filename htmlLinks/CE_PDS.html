“Symptoms: headaches, insomnia, confusion. Diagnosis: acute Ijustdontgetitis!
Exploring the Language and Culture within the Medical Environment”

Date: August 3, 2008 10:00am – 4:00pm Presenter: Jody Gill, CI, CT


WORKSHOP DESCRIPTION:
This workshop will explore the interpersonal, intrapersonal relationships and dynamics, goals of healthcare professionals and the challenges and potential conflicts that arise within the medical environment, including the balancing of boundaries. There will be discussion on how to educate and/or advocate and when NOT to. The presenter will identify resources for additional support and clarification. Environmental challenges and medical terminology will also be addressed.

Educational Objectives:
After completing this workshop, participants will be able to:
  • Identify tools and strategies for managing difficult encounters in the medical environment
• Develop “scripts” to help healthcare personnel understand the interpreter’s function
• More efficiently assess a situation and identify options (in the moment)
• Understand the goals, motives, stresses of healthcare staff
• Recognize medical cultural issues and integrate learning

“Whatchamacallit and Thingamajig: English Expansion for Interpreters”
Date: September 14, 2008 10:00am – 6:00pm Presenter: Amy Meckler, CI


WORKSHOP DESCRIPTION:
Participants will work in groups to explore high register vocabulary and context- and profession-specific vocabulary by paring common words with their high register synonyms, and by determining how word pairs differ by meaning (denotation), register of formality, connotation and context of use. Participants will find common grammar errors in sample sentences and change sentences to reflect standard usage. Participants will also individually practice creating nuanced English sentences and will make short speeches to match a given register on an impromptu topic. The above will be applied to ASL and appropriate possible English interpretations of the source text.

Educational Objectives:
After completing this workshop, participants will:
  • Learn many new high-register words and their synonyms.
• Determine how similar words differ and the correct context for their use.
• Recognize common grammar mistakes made in causal and consultative speech and will learn standard English corrections for such errors.
• Learn numerous English words and phrases for commonly glossed ASL signs.
• Learn how to continue improving their English and learning new vocabulary.

“CDI Study Series: MODULE III – Ethical Decision Making”

Date: July 27, 2008 & August 10, 2008 1:00pm – 6:00pm Presenter: Lewis Merkin, CDI


WORKSHOP DESCRIPTION:
Interpreters will be introduced to the tenets of the Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf Professional Code of Conduct (i.e. Code of Ethics). Emphasis will be placed on an analysis of one’s perspective of values and ethical decision making process. Ethical situations, as experienced in the field of Deaf Interpreters, will be explored and discussed along with ethical business practices.
Educational Objectives:
After completing this workshop, participants will:
  • Understand the important components of the RID Code of Ethics and how they apply to the work of a CDI.
• Identify the strategies to assist in the ethical decision making process while on the job.
• Explore various aspects of ethical business practices and how to apply them to the world of work.
