COURSE DESCRIPTION:
This course presents practical, successful treatment strategies based on current research on the visual vestibular system and the role it plays in development and daily function. Participants will learn to identify signs of visual/vestibular dysfunction, the role of behavioral vision treatment as it relates to your therapy treatment and when to refer clients for vision therapy. They will learn theory, assessment and treatment and how to develop effective treatment protocols for the clinic and school with measurable goals. Treatment and case study videos are presented with a multidisciplinary understanding for improved outcomes. While the majority of the films and case studies are of children, we have also applied these treatments to adults with great success. Patient population includes: TBI, ADD, autism, developmental disabilities, and learning disabilities as well as other developmental issues.

Debra Dickson, RPT is a renowned clinician and is the owner of TherapyWorks Pediatrics, LLC in Old Lyme, CT. She is NDT certified in pediatrics and has been a pediatric therapist for 28 years. Debra has a strong backround in sensory processing, nutrition and immune function as they relate to children with developmental disabilities. She works closely with behavioral optometrists in treating the visual/vestibular system. She lectures both locally and nationally on these topics and sits on the professional advisory committees of several nonprofit agencies. Debra and Anne Buckley-Reen, OTR developed a program for therapists entitled "The Whole Child: S.A.N.E. Strategies for Success" and are currently co-authoring a companion manual for parents by the same title.
