SEMINAR DESCRIPTION:
These two hands-on workshops (two Sundays) will focus on creating workstation
tasks, schedules and communication books for the PDD child and provide case studies and supporting
visuals in the creation of sensory appropriate environments in the classroom, therapy
room, and at home. Most importantly, participants will learn to create a team approach to benefit
children with autism – including teachers, aides, OTs, PTs, SLPs, family members andeveryone else
who may come in contact with these highly sensitive children.

Teachers and therapists will learn to:
• Create schedules, work station tasks and communication books to use in the therapy room,
classroom and home.
• Problem solve for the difficult child or the unusual situation and
• Write more effective IEPs and goals.

The success you see in the children you work with will encourage you to set your goals for them
higher and higher!
