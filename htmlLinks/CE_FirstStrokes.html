SEMINAR DESCRIPTION: This hands-on two day workshop will demonstrate handwriting techniques that can be implemented in any handwriting program, as well as programs geared specifically for children with special needs. Day One will review Therapeutic Intervention for Fine Motor Gymnastics to Multi-sensory Print and Therapeutic Intervention for Fine Motor/Writing Success for Children with Autism and Special Needs. Day Two will focus on Multisensory Cursive, Keyboarding/Assistive Technology and Therapeutic Remedial Print for Older Students. The workshop includes integrated labs, case studies and video demonstrations.

Appropriate for: Occupational Therapists, Teachers (K-6), Special Ed. Teachers, Assistive Technology Specialists

LEARNING OBJECTIVES:
• Review selected research on grasp development and intervention.
• Remedial intervention techniques for letter formation, letter reversals, number reversals, kinesthetic handwriting.
• Identify incorrect grasp patters and learn intervention for specific grasp pattern errors.
• Recognize small group treatment planning for grasp/fine motor skills and handwriting within special populations.
• Practice assessment techniques
• Identify and practice remediation techniques for visual memory, visual cueing, and visual motor integration of handwriting within labs.
• Recognize assistive technology options for students with poor handwriting.
• Recognize on and off activities for multi-sensory keyboarding.
• Discuss “dysgraphia” and other learning challenges, and intervention techniques.

 Jan McCleskey, MA, OTR, is the author of the First Strokes handwriting products and the owner of The Handwriting Clinic in Plano, Texas. Her 23 years experience include 19 years in early childhood and public schools. The Handwriting Clinic serves a variety of populations including students with dyslexia, learning disabilities, vision related learning disabilities, autism, cerebral palsy, hypotonia, mental retardation, delayed fine motor skills, and delayed visual perceptual skill development, as well as students who struggle with handwriting and have no other difficulties in school. She has special interest in and knowledge of assistive technology issues related to handwriting and presents numerous workshops nationwide.
