SEMINAR DESCRIPTION:
This course focuses on working with children who have been diagnosed with and identified as having auditory processing disorders (APD). Divided into three parts, it includes a. A brief overview of how different professions define auditory processing and a presentation of Dr. Lucker’s systems integrative approach; b. Appropriate assessment methods with a focus on a screening tool that is successful, simple and can be used by any professional and accommodations available to children with APD; c. Treatment strategies and activities.

Participants will be able to:
• Identify the five systems that account for how we process auditory information
• Identify each of the seven categories of APD and one specific skill needed in each category
• Describe some of the confounding variables found in typical tests used to assess APD in children by speech-language pathologists, OTs, psychologists and educational specialists
• State for whom specific accommodations like FM systems are appropriate
• Describe at least one activity that can be used in each of the seven different categories of APD

Dr. Jay R. Lucker, often referred to as Dr. J., is a certified audiologist and speech-language pathologist. He received an MA in communication sciences – audiology from Hunter College of the City University of New York and an Ed.D. from Columbia University for his work on auditory perception, learning disabilities, and audiology. Dr. Lucker works in schools, clinics, county health centers, medical offices, ENT physicians’ practices, hospital departments of otolaryngology and pediatrics, and university speech and hearing clinics. In 2001, he opened a private practice specializing in assessment, advocating and intervention services for children and adults with auditory processing and language processing deficits. Dr. Lucker is also a nationally known presenter and a widely published author on the topic of auditory processing disorders.
