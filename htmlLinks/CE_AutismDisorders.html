COURSE DESCRIPTION:
This course will present new research on causes of autism and the differences among syndromes
on the autism spectrum and other disorders of social learning. Assessment and practical outcome
oriented treatment approaches will be the focus. The course will begin with a brief overview of
new genetic and neurological research on etiology, followed by advanced assessment approaches
and intervention strategies including both speech-language and sensory diets and adaptations.
This course is intended for experienced clinicians who would like to increase their understanding
of and treatment options for autism spectrum disorders including PDD-NOS, Asperger's, Rett's
Disorder, and all other disorders on the spectrum. The course review will include such treatment
options as D.I.R., RDI, Rapid Prompting, and ABA.

At the conclusion of the course the participants will:
1. Explain the neurological basis of autism and related processing disorders
2. Differentially diagnose autism spectrum disorders
3. Develop evidence-based interventions for the full range of autism spectrum disorders
