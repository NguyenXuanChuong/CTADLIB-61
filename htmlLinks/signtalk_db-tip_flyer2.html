OVERVIEW:

Handshapes are one of the five fundamental building blocks of a sign: handshape, movement, location, orientation, and nonmanual markers. Hearing interpreters are often ill-equipped to provide clear representation in ASL through the use of all of the types of classifiers that are available in ASL. This webinar will provide a strong foundation of three of the major categories of classifiers: descriptive, locative, and element. Modeling of various classifiers will assist with skill acquisition. Upon completion of the webinar,participants can anticipate being more comfortable with the production of classifiers in all categories.



This Class will be conducted in ASL



EDUCATIONAL OBJECTIVES:

Upon completion of the session participants will be able to:

• Demonstrate knowledge of the rules of classifiers in ASL.
• Differentiate between different types of classifiers, such as element, descriptive and locative
• Recognize when and how to use classifiers to clarify meaning in ASL (i.e. share information about the surroundings; and/or as part of a story, to set the scene or to share characteristics, etc.)
